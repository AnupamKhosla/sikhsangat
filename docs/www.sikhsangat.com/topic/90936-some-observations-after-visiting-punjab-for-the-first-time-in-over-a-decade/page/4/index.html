    Had a weird experience at a new job I was on (was only a one dayer). I was the only brown face there bar one freshie with a pagh. Then he did it again, and I said to him: "I'm from malwa, you're from majha, why are you speaking hindi with me??" I don't know what he mumbled in response. Something like "I do both."

    I tell you' I've met a few desi pendu labourers  on sites lately that have been f**king lazy. Me and this guy didn't really get on. I hope I don't have to work with him much. 

    Another thing I realised about freshy pandus, is if you try and help them with their labourer workload from the kindness of your heart, the panchoday try and run off and duck doing the work altogether, lazy, sneaky  peasants.

    No, Hindi is not more refined. It's just that Punjabis themselves are not refined.

    I do understand that there are variations in the "feel" of languages: say, French vs. German.

    But there's barely a difference grammatically and syntactically Punjabi vs. Hindi.

    Punjabis could speak Punjabi in a refined manner, too. But they don't.
