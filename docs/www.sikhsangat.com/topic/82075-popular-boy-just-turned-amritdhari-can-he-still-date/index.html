Idealy I think he might not be dating because it does not have much meaning for an amritdhari. An amritdhari takes amrit in order to become closer to Guru ji and the all divine Waheguru. Technically if he falls back in his old ways he might not be regarded as amritdhari.  

People are allowed to change.  Depending on the way he carries himself he might encourage both boys and girls to become amritdhari or he might turn the other way. 

Only time can tell. Sometimes we do underestimate people.  He might be a better Sikh than all the rest of us. Talking to the opposite gender does not necessarily mean that something evil is going on, as assumed.

It takes  strong willed and full of character person to make the decision he has taken. I mean it both before and after amrit. He could be simply be an outgoing person whose company people do enjoy. One of my nephews is similar to him.

VJKK VJKF

Technically, he is not allowed to date whether the girl is amritdhari or not because in Sikhi there is no such thing as dating. Maharaaj says that its much better to get into a relationship properly once. (Marriage). As for his facial features, Vaheguru made him so shouldn't you love Vaheguru? I am not anti-dating but I am just telling you from a gurbani point of view. The panj chor are coming into play and they will find a way to enter your body and thoughts. Having lustful thoughts about someone is hard to control because the panj chor are much stronger than us and Vaheguru made the panj chor as a test to see who can control them and who will have conquered them.

barahm gi-aanee kai aykai rang.

The God-conscious being loves the One Lord alone.

Because I am practising to take amrit (and even before that) I have never ever dated anyone because I know that its wrong. By dating you are not being true to your future spouse because you have previously been in a relationship and you have already done wrong things with each other within that fake relationship. If other people want to date then they can but as Sikhs of the Guru we have every right to know what our Guru said to us. Whatever our Guru said to us was purely for our best, the Guru never wanted us to be led astray. By dating we are also conforming to society and being "normal" in our friends and peers eyes. If we look in the mirror and we look like everyone else then there is a big problem because Guru Ji says:

ਜਬ ਲਗ ਖ਼ਾਲਸਾ ਰਹੇ ਨਿਆਰਾ ॥ jab lag Khalsa rehae niara While the Khalsa stays unique and distinct,
ਤਬ ਲਗ ਤੇਜ ਕੀਉ ਮੈਂ ਸਾਰਾ ॥ thab lag thaej keeo main sara I will offer my full support
ਜਬ ਇਹ ਗਹੈ ਬਿਪਰਨ ਕੀ ਰੀਤ ॥ jab eih gehai biparan kee reeth When they follow the ways of others,
ਮੈਂ ਨ ਕਰੋਂ ਇਨ ਕੀ ਪ੝ਰਤੀਤ ॥ main n karon ein kee pratheeth I will withdraw my assistance from them.

Its there in a bani called Khalsa Mahima. As I said before I am not against dating but I am just telling you what my Guru said. The Guru's opinion should be in almost every decision you make in life because you know that it will always be true because the Guru's were Nirankaar themselves:

Gur parmesar eko jaan||                                                                                                                                                                                      Know that Guru and Vaheguru are one.

Bhull Chuk Maaf.

VJKK VJKF
